Best Practices

Many threats exist in the cyber-space today and more often than not, the areas we overlook may make us victims. The following sections briefly summarize how to safely navigate the web and how to prepare yourself for a potential breach of information.

Passwords

Password safety is a very rudimentary step in the world of computers today. When creating a password, a user who can make a password they can easily remember may not always be the safest. Passwords should be sophisticated and reasonably lengthy. A rule of thumb for passwords is to make them long. The Cybersecurity & Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) recommends using atleast 16 characters for password creation. In addition to using sophisticated and long passwords, CISA recommends using a password manager to keep track and secure this information.

CISA.gov published a video for best practices on their website to guide everyday users with their recommendations on this topic.

Software Updates

Growing up in the 2000s-2010s, I can remember a time when Windows Defender was known for being a mediocre form of protection out-of-the-box for Windows XP users. Thankfully, times have changed and proprietary OS security features now hold their own weight. Security updates are a necessity for staying secure today, even if you use 3rd party security and antivirus software. Keeping your Operating System and software up-to-date is critical for preventing potentially dangerous exploits occurring on your devices. This does not only apply to computers, as cellphones nearly mirror the functions of a computer today, they should also be up to date. While I cannot assume what OS or device you use, ensuring you are using the most recent version of your required software is paramount in today's dynamic world of threats.

Phishing

Phishing is a social engineering method of deceiving a person that the representation of something is the real thing. It's fraud for the digital space. In today's age, phishing is used to obtain credentials, personal information, financial data, and so on. This may come in the form of an email, text, social media, and anywhere where you can be communicated to. The simplest way to avoid being 'phished' is to identify possible hints of any strange nature. Phishing is not restricted to personal use. Phishing occurs in the work space and may compromise a larger network if security procedures are lacking. Antivirus Software

Antivirus Software is an essential form of software, especially for users who have reason to browse a lot on the web, or have a lot of crucial information to keep secure on their device. Antivirus software is especially useful for preventing potentially malicious software from being accessed, but it is even more useful when an infection has compromised your system. This safeguard is comparable to getting a vaccine, but for your devices. There are plenty of reputable Antivirus companies that exist who make great products. I recommend doing the research to establish your needs and be informed to make that small investment.

Firewalls

Firewalls in short, prevent certain traffic from the web to access your system. With all types of data containing many identifiers and port numbers, you can set up a firewall with either the features that come with your OS (such as Windows) or with software you can obtain such as an antivirus. This software-firewall is the most convenient form of firewall to most users at home. Enterprise networks and confidential systems may use a physical-firewall that can prevent more traffic or even perform more sophisticated tasks beyond the scope of this section. In a nutshell, your firewall will act as a filter and reject potentially unwanted traffic.
